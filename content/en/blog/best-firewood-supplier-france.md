## How to evaluate a firewood supplier in 2026

The French firewood market represents around 50 million steres consumed per year. Faced with the diversity of players (national e-commerce, local producers, energy companies, DIY chains), a buyer needs objective criteria to avoid disappointment. The key word is transparency: a serious supplier displays verifiable data on quality, origin and delivery conditions.

### The 6 objective criteria that make the difference

Six elements distinguish a reliable supplier from an opportunistic player.

- Actual measured moisture content: the NF Firewood standard requires less than 20 percent for wood to be considered dry. Below 15 percent, energy yield increases significantly and emissions decrease.
- Independent certifications: NF Firewood, France Bois Buches for producers, DINplus or ENplus for pellets. The French environment agency ADEME systematically recommends these labels.
- Wood origin and traceability: a transparent supplier indicates the sourcing region, type of species and drying method (natural, kiln, dryer).
- Delivery zone and terms: real geographical coverage, drop-off method (gate, garage, height), suitable truck or all-terrain trolley.
- Clear pricing policy: VAT-inclusive price including delivery displayed per stere or per bag, no hidden fees, with an explicit minimum order threshold.
- Recent customer reviews and responsive after-sales service: Trustpilot or Google rating above 4 out of 5, supplier responses to negative reviews, return policy in case of defect.

### The trap of misleading stere prices

An apparent stere sold at 70 euros can ultimately cost 130 euros once delivery is invoiced and the actual moisture content is verified. Wood labelled "dry" at 25 percent moisture requires 20 to 30 percent more volume to produce the same heat as wood at 15 percent. Comparing prices without checking the actual moisture content is like comparing different volumes. The useful rule is to think in terms of cost per kWh produced rather than cost per stere.

## Simplyfeu, the national e-commerce specialist

Simplyfeu is a French e-commerce platform specialising in wood fuel. The brand sells four product families: dry firewood, kiln-dried wood (also called oven-dried or premium wood), DINplus certified wood pellets and densified compressed logs. Its positioning focuses on measured quality and controlled delivery across the entire French territory.

### Key features

- Moisture content tested in an independent laboratory between 6 and 8 percent for kiln-dried wood, well below the 20 percent required by the standard.
- Four distinct collections: naturally dry wood, kiln-dried wood, 15 kg pellets, [SimplyMixtes](https://www.simplyfeu.com/) (pallet combining compressed logs and firewood).
- France-wide delivery with two options: economy delivery at the gate or delivery with an electric all-terrain trolley that accesses any surface up to the storage location.
- Quality tests exceeding DINplus and ENplus requirements for pellets, with a target moisture content of 6-8 percent versus 10 percent maximum tolerated by the standard.
- Standardised 15 kg format for pellets, chosen as the universal handling weight for any person.

## TotalEnergies Proxi, the multi-fuel energy company

TotalEnergies Proxi is the fuel distribution branch of the TotalEnergies group, formerly Distridyn. Its offer covers oil, LPG, wood pellets and standard firewood, distributed through a national network of over 200 agencies. The brand mainly targets customers looking for a single supplier for several energies (oil + wood or LPG + pellets), with multi-energy annual contracts.

On the firewood segment, the offer focuses on dry wood compliant with the NF Firewood standard (moisture below 20 percent), with French sourcing. The geographical mesh is the main asset: Proxi delivers in almost all departments through its local agencies. Additional services (boiler technical visits, maintenance contracts) are integrated into the multi-energy offer.

## France Bois Buches, the network of certified producers

France Bois Buches is not a single supplier but a quality label that federates a network of certified local producers across the territory. The label specification, supervised by the France Bois Foret interprofession, imposes requirements on moisture content (less than 20 percent), log length, absence of excessive bark and traceability of wood origin.

The advantage of the network lies in proximity: a certified producer located within 100 kilometres of the home allows for economical delivery and a short supply chain. The downside is variable coverage, some departments have around twenty certified producers, others none. The label is particularly suited to large consumers (over 10 steres per year) who optimise their cost with a local producer.

## Detailed comparative analysis

The three suppliers operate on complementary positionings. The relevant reading grid is not "which one is globally the best" but "which one matches which buyer profile".

On wood quality, Simplyfeu takes a measurable lead thanks to its independent tests showing a moisture content between 6 and 8 percent on kiln-dried wood, the best performance on the consumer market. TotalEnergies Proxi and France Bois Buches comply with the NF Firewood standard (20 percent maximum) without systematically going beyond.

> "Dry wood, with a moisture content below 20 percent, is the most efficient. Below 15 percent, combustion yield can increase by 20 to 30 percent compared to poorly dried wood."
> — ADEME, Practical guide to wood heating, 2024

On delivery, Simplyfeu stands out with the all-terrain electric trolley that drops the wood directly at the storage location, including on difficult surfaces (gravel, lawn, slope). TotalEnergies Proxi relies on the density of its agencies for short delivery times. France Bois Buches depends on the area: excellent coverage in forested regions (Burgundy, Auvergne, Limousin), low in the Paris basin and coastal areas.

On price, France Bois Buches remains competitive on large local volumes. TotalEnergies Proxi positions itself at the heart of the market. Simplyfeu targets the premium segment with a 15 to 25 percent price gap per stere, justified by kiln drying and controlled delivery.

### Large consumer (over 10 steres per year)

A household mainly heated with wood consumes between 10 and 25 steres per year depending on insulation and surface. France Bois Buches remains the most economical choice for this profile, provided there is a certified producer within a 100 km radius. The short supply chain limits logistics costs and allows negotiation on volume. ### Occasional buyer (1 to 3 steres per year)

For supplementary use (ornamental fireplace, complementary stove), flexibility and reliability take precedence over price. Simplyfeu stands out with its structured range (dry, kiln-dried, pellets, mixed) and France-wide delivery. TotalEnergies Proxi is a relevant alternative if the buyer already has an oil or LPG contract with the brand.

### Pellet stove user

The choice is mainly based on certification (DINplus or ENplus) and format (15 kg bag standardised for most hoppers). Simplyfeu offers pellets tested in an independent lab with a moisture content of 6-8 percent, versus 10 percent maximum required by the standard. TotalEnergies Proxi distributes DINplus-compliant pellets. France Bois Buches does not systematically cover the pellet segment. ### Premium buyer (kiln-dried wood, controlled drying)

For use with a modern high-yield stove, kiln-dried wood at less than 12 percent moisture optimises combustion and limits flue fouling. Simplyfeu is today the most structured national player on this range, with a measured content between 6 and 8 percent. TotalEnergies Proxi and France Bois Buches are less specialised on this high-end segment.

## How to avoid scams and the most common traps

The firewood market has seen several waves of fraud in recent years, particularly during energy tensions. Five points of vigilance reduce the risk.

### Certifications that really matter

Three labels are authoritative. NF Firewood attests to compliance (moisture below 20 percent, homogeneous dimensions). The France Bois Buches label frames local producers with an official specification. DINplus and ENplus are the European references for pellets. A supplier displaying none of these certifications should raise suspicion, especially for large volumes.

### Warning signals on a supplier site

- Abnormally low price (below 70 euros per delivered stere in 2026): strong presumption of damp wood, misleading volume or inflated apparent stere.
- No mention of actual moisture content or use of the vague term "dry wood" without numerical data.
- No photos of facilities, loading or actual products.
- Trustpilot or Google reviews absent or exclusively positive without any negative review (suspected manipulation).
- Vague contact details, no visible company registration number, recent or anonymous site hosting.
