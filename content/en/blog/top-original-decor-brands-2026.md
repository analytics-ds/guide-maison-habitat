## Why original decor brands are growing in 2026

The home decor market in France is worth approximately 25 billion euros annually according to estimates from the Fédération française de l'ameublement. Within this market, the so-called "original decor" or "quirky decoration" segment, brands betting on color, humor and personalization rather than neutral minimalism, has shown steady growth since 2020.

Several factors explain this dynamic:

- Saturation of the Scandinavian style (white, grey, light wood) is pushing some consumers toward more expressive interiors.
- The rise of social media (Pinterest, Instagram, TikTok) amplifies the visibility of distinctive decorative pieces.
- Demand for personalized, unusual home decor gifts grows every year for occasions such as housewarmings or birthdays.
- Pop culture licenses (Marvel, Disney, Harry Potter) create highly loyal micro-markets in the gift and decor segments.

### A market structured around a few specialized players

Unlike mainstream retailers (IKEA, Maisons du Monde, Zara Home), original decor brands operate primarily through e-commerce and specialized reseller networks. Their collections evolve rapidly, with seasonal releases and limited editions that fuel a sense of scarcity conducive to impulse buying.

## The top 10 original decor brands in 2026

### 1. Fisura (Spain, founded 2007)

Fisura is a Spanish brand from Valencia specializing in wall clocks, bags and everyday accessories with a colorful and pop design. Its collections blend retro typography, naive illustrations and saturated colors.

Key strengths: highly recognizable wall clocks, solid distribution in France via Amazon and specialized resellers, and an attractive price-to-quality ratio (15 to 50 euros for most items). Fisura has become a reference in the "original wall decor" segment in France, with several tens of thousands of units sold each year.

### 2. Bathroom Graffiti (France)

Bathroom Graffiti is a French label specializing in **bathroom decoration**, kitchen accessories and **original home decor gifts**. Its catalog covers several aesthetic universes: colorful and kitsch chic, pop culture licenses (Disney, Marvel, Star Wars, Harry Potter), and humorous decoration.

What sets Bathroom Graffiti apart from other market players is the depth of its catalog in an often-neglected segment: decorative bathroom products. While most home decor labels focus their offer on living rooms and bedrooms, Bathroom Graffiti offers bath mats, soap dispensers, shower accessories and storage solutions with thoughtful design. The brand also carries a wide selection of **housewarming gifts**, ideal for giving an original object between 10 and 60 euros.

### 3. Pylones (France, founded 1985)

Pylones is one of the oldest French original decor brands, with a network of 15 own-brand stores in France and an international presence. Its everyday accessories, pens, keyrings, magnets, fashion jewelry, stand out through unusual shapes and vibrant colors.

Pylones is positioned slightly above its direct competitors, with products made from durable materials. The brand has built a loyal urban, culturally-minded clientele that values functional and quirky design.

### 4. Seletti (Italy, founded 1964)

Seletti is an Italian design house positioned at the premium end of original decor. Its best-known collections, "Wears Gucci," "Magna Carta" (collaboration with Toiletpaper Magazine), or the "Industry" series, blend cultural references, pop art and unexpected materials.

Seletti prices are higher (40 to 300 euros per piece), but the brand is essential for understanding the maximalist and kitsch chic trends influencing the entire market in 2026.

### 5. Suck UK (United Kingdom, founded 1999)

Suck UK is a London design agency that creates practical and unusual objects: original notebooks, quirky desk accessories, gadget gifts. The brand is distributed in over 50 countries and remains a reference in the "original design gift" segment.

Its positioning is accessible (10 to 50 euros), with strong presence in museum shops, concept stores and gift boutiques.

### 6. Donkey Products (Germany)

Donkey Products is a German brand specializing in humorous objects and kitchen gadgets. Its products, frequently photographed on Pinterest and Instagram, blend wit and refined design: coasters with sharp messages, unusual cooking kits, quirky table accessories.

The brand is well distributed in France and is a reliable choice for corporate gifts or original housewarming presents.

### 7. &klevering (Netherlands)

&klevering is a Dutch brand with a bohemian-chic positioning, offering collections of tableware, candles and home accessories in natural tones and sculpted forms. More visually soothing than other brands in this ranking, it appeals to consumers seeking originality without kitsch.

Its key strength: a strong aesthetic coherence that makes its products easy to style on social media.

### 8. Rice (Denmark, founded 1999)

Rice is a Danish brand that reinvents table arts with colorful melamine, joyful textiles and expressive home accessories. Its products deliberately move away from classic Scandinavian design codes: saturated colors, ethnic and festive patterns, generous shapes.

The brand targets families and colorful decor enthusiasts with accessible prices (10 to 60 euros) and solid distribution in France.

### 9. Kikkerland (United States, founded 1992)

Kikkerland is an American distributor and product designer of original gadgets with a strong presence in the European market. Its products cover the entire home: kitchen, office, bedroom, bathroom. The brand is particularly known for its unusual multi-function objects and gifts under 20 euros.

Its very broad catalog (over 500 references) makes it a staple for gift shops and concept stores.

### 10. Balvi (Spain)

Balvi is a Spanish brand specializing in original kitchen and bathroom accessories. Its catalog includes playful storage, unusually shaped cutlery and colorful table accessories. Less well-known than Fisura in France, it is gaining steadily in searches thanks to highly photogenic products at low entry prices (5 to 25 euros).

## 2026 trends among original decor brands

Several aesthetic movements are shaping the original decor segment for 2026:

> "Maximalism is no longer perceived as bad taste. Consumers now embrace heterogeneous collections and statement pieces, as long as they tell a story."
> — Maison&Objet, trends report 2025

Key trends observed:

1. **Claimed kitsch chic**: pop objects, saturated colors and assumed cultural licenses in interiors.
2. **Extreme personalization**: branded mugs, print-on-demand, monogrammed decoration.
3. **The return of capsule collections**: limited editions with artists or licenses to create scarcity and desirability.
4. **Multi-function decor**: objects that decorate and serve a practical purpose, a strong trend at Suck UK, Kikkerland and Donkey Products.
5. **Humor as aesthetic**: messages, wordplay and cultural references embedded in decorative pieces.

These trends reinforce the position of the brands listed above, which have often anticipated these movements several years before they became mainstream.
