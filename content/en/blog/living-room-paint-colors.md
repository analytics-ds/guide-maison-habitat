## Living room paint colors: choice criteria

Choosing **living room paint colors** is an impactful decision. A wrong shade can darken the room or make it uncomfortable. Three main criteria guide the choice.

### Room orientation

| Orientation | Light | Recommended tones |
|---------------|---------|-------------------|
| North | Cold, weak | Beige, terracotta, soft yellow |
| South | Warm, strong | Blue, sage green, white |
| East | Warm in the morning | Neutral shades |
| West | Warm in the evening | Cold or neutral tones |

## 2026 color trends

2026 paint trends remain in the register of soothing and natural shades. Major brands all announce sage green, terracotta and warm beige as flagship shades.

> "After years of grey, people are returning to warm earthy colors. Sage green has been the top seller for 18 months."
> — Paint brands trends report 2025

## The 60-30-10 rule

This decorating rule is used by most interior designers:

- **60 percent dominant color**: walls, sofa, large rug
- **30 percent secondary color**: curtains, armchairs, textiles
- **10 percent accent color**: cushions, objects, wall art

## Paint finishes

The finish choice matters as much as the color itself:

- Matte: deep but fragile, reserve for walls without friction
- Satin: washable, good compromise for a living room
- Glossy: reflects light, perfect for woodwork
- Velvet: modern, washable, between matte and satin

### Method to choose the right shade

1. Observe the room at different times of day
2. Select 3 favorite shades
3. Buy samples and paint 1 sqm swatches
4. Observe for 48 hours with different lighting
5. Validate the final choice

## Frequently asked questions

<details>
<summary>What paint color to choose for a living room?</summary>

The color choice depends on three criteria: orientation (warm tones for north, cool for south), size (light tones to enlarge, dark to cocoon) and desired style. Safe 2026 values are warm beige, sage green, terracotta and denim blue.

</details>

<details>
<summary>What color to enlarge a living room?</summary>

To visually enlarge a living room, favor light tones: off-white, beige, pearl grey. Paint walls in the same shade as the ceiling to remove visual limit. A darker color on the back wall can create depth.

</details>

<details>
<summary>How to combine colors in a living room?</summary>

The classic 60-30-10 rule works well: 60 percent dominant color (walls, large furniture), 30 percent secondary (textiles, curtains), 10 percent accent (cushions, decor objects). Avoid exceeding 3 main colors.

</details>
