> **In brief:**
> 1. In a small living room under 15 sqm, limit furniture to 3 large pieces maximum to keep circulation clear.
> 2. Light colors reflect up to 80 percent of light and visually enlarge the room.
> 3. Vertical storage (high shelves, column furniture) frees floor space and adds perceived ceiling height.
> 4. A large mirror placed opposite a light source visually doubles the perceived volume.

## Designing a small living room: the basics

A small living room under 15 sqm requires thinking about every square meter. Contrary to popular belief, **designing a small living room** does not mean sacrificing comfort: just choose less but better.

### Measure before buying

Before any purchase, draw a plan to 1/50 scale. Record wall dimensions, windows and radiators. This avoids bad surprises on delivery day.

## Choosing suitable furniture

The sofa is the central piece. Choose a 2-seater model from 180 to 200 cm, compact but comfortable. Sofas with built-in storage are valuable in a small space.

### Dimensions to remember

| Furniture | Maximum dimension |
|-----------|---------------------|
| 2-seater sofa | 200 cm long |
| Coffee table | 80 cm diameter |
| TV unit | 120 cm long |

## Playing with colors and light

Light tones are your allies. Off-white or beige reflects light and visually pushes back walls. To avoid monotony, add color through cushions, artwork or an accent wall.

## Optimizing storage

Vertical storage is the key. High shelves, column bookcases, corner units: everything that goes up frees floor space. A full wall of storage can advantageously replace several scattered small pieces.

### Steps to choose well

1. Identify objects to store and their volume
2. Measure available ceiling height
3. Choose floor-to-ceiling integrated storage
4. Leave breathing space above the sofa
