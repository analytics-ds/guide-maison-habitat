## Why the brasero stands out in event show cooking

**Show cooking on a brasero** turns a reception into a full culinary experience. Wood-fire cooking on a circular plancha creates a warm moment: the crackling of the embers, the flavors of the grill and the staging of cooking in front of guests animate a wedding, a birthday or a corporate party. For a caterer, it is a differentiating asset that enhances the service and makes it memorable.

But event use imposes constraints a garden brasero does not face. The equipment must **transport and assemble quickly** from one location to another, withstand an **intensive service pace**, accommodate a **large number of guests** and guarantee impeccable food safety. These criteria drive the choice of a professional event brasero.

### The three key criteria for a caterer

- **Capacity**: a wedding or corporate reception often gathers 50 to 100 people. The plancha diameter determines the number of guests served on a single hearth.
- **Transport and assembly**: a brasero that assembles without screws or tools loads, moves and installs in a few minutes at the event venue, a decisive time-saver in service.
- **Durability and hygiene**: a thick steel plancha compliant with food contact handles repeated temperature rises and cleans easily between two services.

## Capacity, transport and budget: sizing your brasero correctly

Choosing a brasero for events first comes down to **serving capacity**. Count about 12 to 15 cm of cooking surface per person: an 82 cm model comfortably covers up to 60 guests, a 98 to 100 cm format allows serving up to 100 people on a single hearth. For a caterer, it is better to plan a capacity margin than to multiply cooking stations.

**Transport and assembly** are the second decisive criterion. A brasero that assembles without screws or tools, like the COEO TRIO, loads and installs in a few minutes, whereas a model to assemble wastes precious time on each service. Also check the empty weight and footprint for loading into a vehicle.

On **budget**, count between 850 and 1,000 euros for a backup model, 1,300 to 1,800 euros for a versatile French brasero sized for large receptions, and over 3,000 euros for a high-end modular pro setup. For a regular business, buying a durable model pays off quickly compared to repeated rental.

## Setting up the cooking area around the brasero

A great show cooking is not limited to the brasero. To streamline service, plan a **side table or worktop** resistant to heat and weather (compact HPL, steel), within reach for mise en place and plating. Cooking accessories, a motorized rotisserie spit for large pieces, a barbecue grill, a tabletop oven for pizzas or a reversible cast iron plate, turn a single hearth into several successive stations and enrich the service.
