## Why buy your wood pellets online in France

The French **wood pellet** market reached approximately 2.3 million tons consumed in 2023 according to Propellet (national wood pellet association), driven by the growth of the pellet stove fleet, estimated at over 2 million installed appliances. This demand has fueled a dense online offer, with four distributor profiles: wood fuel specialists, multi-energy distributors, DIY chains and local producers.

Online purchasing offers three concrete advantages for a French household. Price comparison between sellers is immediate, which is not the case in stores or with local producers. Pallet delivery saves the handling of more than 60 bags from a store to the home. Finally, early orders between April and July benefit from off-season rates, sometimes 15 to 20 percent lower than November and December prices.

### Objective criteria to compare sites

Five criteria allow rational comparison of pellet sales sites online:

- **Certification**: ENplus A1, DINplus or NF Granules biocombustibles, present on the product sheet and on the delivered bag.
- **Calorific value**: greater than or equal to 4.6 kWh per kilo for ENplus A1.
- **Ash content**: below 0.7 percent for A1, below 1.2 percent for A2.
- **Packaging**: standard 15 kg bag, 65-bag pallet (975 kg) or bulk depending on needs.
- **Delivery conditions**: covered zone, announced lead time, included or separate delivery fee, tail lift availability.

## Practical tips for buying wood pellets online

Five tips help optimize an online pellet purchase, regardless of the chosen site.

Anticipate orders between April and July, an off-season period when prices are generally 10 to 20 percent lower than November and December prices. This anticipation requires a dry and ventilated storage space, ideally sheltered from weather, capable of accommodating 975 kg on pallet for 6 to 12 months.

Check certification on the product sheet before ordering: ENplus A1 (European standard), DINplus (German equivalent) or NF Granules biocombustibles (French AFNOR standard). Delivered bags must carry the corresponding logo. In the absence of a logo, do not order.

Compare delivered prices, not product prices. Some sites display a low price and then bill delivery, which can represent 50 to 80 euros per pallet. Delivered price is the only comparable figure.

Consult recent reviews (last 12 months) on Trustpilot or Google. At least 50 recent reviews with an average rating above 4 out of 5 constitute a positive signal. A rating below 3.5 or recurring complaints about lead times or product quality should raise concerns.

Test with one pallet before committing to several annual orders. Quality may vary from one batch to another, even with identical certification. A test order validates the actual quality before a more important commitment.

### Mistakes to avoid

1. Ordering in November or December without anticipation: peak prices, extended lead times due to high demand.
2. Ignoring certification: a non-certified pellet can have an ash content two or three times higher, increasing fouling and stove maintenance frequency.
3. Comparing pre-tax prices instead of delivered tax-included prices: delivery represents 50 to 80 euros per pallet, or 10 to 15 percent of the total price.
4. Storing pellets in a humid area: a moisture content above 10 percent degrades pellets and reduces calorific value.
5. Choosing a site without checking the delivery zone: some sites announce mainland France but exclude isolated areas or charge a surcharge.
