## Why buy compressed wood logs online in France

Wood energy remains the leading source of renewable energy consumed in France, ahead of wind, solar and hydroelectric. This structural position has supported the growth of the densified log market, which now represents a credible alternative to traditional log wood for households equipped with a wood stove, insert or fireplace. The online offer has expanded at a sustained pace since 2020, with four dominant profiles: wood specialists, multi-energy operators, large DIY chains and regional producers.

Online purchase offers three concrete benefits for a French household. Price comparison between players becomes immediate, which remains impossible in retail or with local producers. Pallet delivery saves the manual transport of 60 to 100 bags from a store to the storage location. Finally, early orders between April and July benefit from off-season prices, sometimes 15 to 20 percent lower than November and December prices.

### The advantages of compressed logs over traditional wood

Compressed wood logs, also called densified logs or densified wood, are manufactured from untreated wood sawdust and chips, compressed under high pressure without any chemical binder. This 100 percent natural manufacturing gives them three measurable advantages over traditional logs.

The calorific value, measured in kWh per kilogram, reaches 4.6 to 5 kWh/kg compared to about 3 kWh/kg for a classic seasoned log, an energy yield 3 times higher for the same volume. A single compressed log delivers the heat of 3 traditional logs.

The moisture content, which determines combustion quality, peaks at 10 percent to comply with DINplus standard, and drops to 6 to 8 percent at the most demanding manufacturers. By comparison, a traditional log dried for 2 years still shows 15 to 20 percent moisture.

Storage footprint, finally, is divided by 4 compared to traditional wood for the same amount of energy produced. A pallet of compressed logs of 1000 kilos replaces the equivalent of 3 cords of traditional wood, which transforms the storage logic for households with little space.

### The 5 criteria to choose a reliable site

Five criteria allow to rationally compare sites selling **compressed wood logs** online:

- **Certification**: displayed presence of at least one DINplus, ENplus label or independent laboratory control (CERIC) on the product sheet and delivered pallet
- **Moisture content**: value shown on the sheet, ideally below 10 percent, with clear advantage under 8 percent
- **Delivery area and lead time**: mainland France coverage, standard lead time below 10 working days, drop-off slot management
- **Wood origin transparency**: species used (hardwoods, softwoods, mix), geographical provenance, sustainable forest management
- **Customer service and after-sales**: French phone line, pallet dispute handling, policy in case of punctured or defective bags

## How delivery of compressed wood logs ordered online works

Standard delivery is by truck under 19 tons, equipped with a hydraulic tail lift and a manual pallet truck. The driver drops the filmed pallet as close as possible to the home, on a flat and accessible area. A simple paved or stabilized driveway suffices in most cases. The carrier usually calls 24 to 48 hours in advance to set a 4-hour slot.

Standard lead time is 5 to 10 working days in mainland France, sometimes 7 to 15 days in winter peak (October to December). Mountain zones or poorly served communes may require an additional 3 to 5 days, as well as delivery by shorter truck adapted to narrow streets.

Drive pickup at depot remains possible with some specialists, including Simplyfeu, with a reduced fee of 50 to 100 euros per pallet. This option requires a vehicle capable of transporting a pallet of 800 to 1000 kilos, a utility vehicle or adapted trailer. For an urban household without a heavy vehicle, home delivery remains the most practical solution.

A frequently overlooked point: delivered bags may show light tears related to transport. These punctures do not affect log quality, which are designed to be stored in open-air silos during manufacturing. A wide piece of tape is enough to reseal a damaged bag without impact on combustion.

## Which compressed wood logs for your usage profile?

The right choice of compressed wood logs depends on three variables: usage frequency, annual volume consumed and storage constraint. Three profiles emerge from French market data.

### For occasional auxiliary heating

Typical profile: an open fireplace or insert used on winter evenings, a few weekend fires, for atmosphere as much as for heat. Annual consumption remains below 500 kilos.

Recommendation: a mixed pack of day logs, in single bags of 5 logs or half-pallet. Prioritize DINplus certified logs to avoid excessive smoke in open fireplaces, and test 2 or 3 brands before ordering in bulk. Simplyfeu offers single bags available without full pallet commitment, which suits this profile.

### For main daily heating

Typical profile: a wood stove or insert used as main heating from October to April, 8 to 12 hours per day. Annual consumption is between 1500 and 3000 kilos, 2 to 3 pallets.

Recommendation: a combination of day logs (combustion 1h30 to 2h) for active phases and night logs (combustion 6 to 8h) for evening loads. Order in full pallets between April and July to capture off-season rates. Prioritize a specialist player like Simplyfeu, which has the manufacturing consistency needed for a full season.

### For apartment use or small storage

Typical profile: an apartment equipped with an approved wood stove, or a house without a cellar or garage large enough for a pallet. Available storage is limited to 300 to 500 kilos.

Recommendation: order in single bags or half-pallet, with quarterly restocking rather than an annual pallet. Since compressed logs are 4 times less bulky than traditional wood for equivalent energy, they particularly suit this profile. The storage rule remains valid: avoid direct contact with water (wet floor, damp wall) while atmospheric humidity has no significant impact on densified logs.
