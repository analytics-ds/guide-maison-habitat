## Why Yvelines is a strategic department for new-build real estate in Île-de-France

### Versailles, Saint-Germain-en-Laye and Sartrouville, three structuring hubs

According to trouver-un-logement-neuf.com, for the period from 22 May 2025 to 22 May 2026, **Versailles concentrates 24.8 % of new-build buying intentions** in Yvelines, followed by Saint-Germain-en-Laye (23.6 %) and Sartrouville (14.9 %). Together, these three towns represent 63.3 % of the department's new-build market. Versailles benefits from UNESCO heritage status and a dense tertiary employment base. Saint-Germain-en-Laye is served by the RER A direct to La Défense and central Paris. Sartrouville, more affordable in price, benefits from a direct Transilien line L connection to Paris Saint-Lazare and an active urban regeneration program.

### A predominantly residential department, with 77 % houses and 76.77 % owner-occupiers

The department counts **77.06 % houses and 76.77 % owner-occupiers**, the most family-oriented market in Île-de-France after the outer suburbs. This structure attracts a different buyer profile than the 92 or central Paris: space, garden, family life, proximity to schools and quick access to Paris. New-build apartment programs still represent 90.5 % of new-build buying intentions, a sign that new households are looking for more affordable, lower-maintenance products than the standalone family home. For a wider comparison between territories, the guide on [country or city: which to choose](/en/blog/country-or-city-choose/) details the lifestyle trade-offs.

### A market 32 % more expensive than the national average, driven by transport

As of 23 February 2025, the Yvelines department showed a price per m2 **32 % above the French national average** (3,041 euros per m2). The average reaches 4,586 euros per m2 for apartments and 3,770 euros per m2 for houses. This valuation is driven by the quality of the transport network: RER A (Saint-Germain-en-Laye, Chatou, Le Vésinet), Transilien L and N, the Eole extension of the RER E to Mantes-la-Jolie by the end of 2026, and the future Line 18 of the Grand Paris Express that will connect Versailles to Orly via the Saclay plateau. ## Interconstruction

### Over 60 years of expertise and a strong positioning on subsidised home ownership in the 78

Founded in 1960 and now a subsidiary of the Eaglestone group, **Interconstruction** delivers between 700 and 750 homes per year. The developer relies on an active presence in Yvelines with three programs in commercialisation: Les Terrasses de l'Hôtel de Ville in Conflans-Sainte-Honorine (early access), Les Ateliers de Poissy (works in progress) and L'Envol in Voisins-le-Bretonneux (commercialisation in progress, from 289,000 euros, combining open-market and Bail Réel Solidaire social leasehold). It is the only developer on this list to concentrate 100 % of its activity on Île-de-France, which translates into a deep understanding of local zoning rules and heritage constraints.

### Accessible programs, from social leasehold to open-market ownership

Interconstruction's positioning in the 78 specifically targets first-time buyers and buyers sensitive to subsidised schemes. The Bail Réel Solidaire social leasehold, managed by the Yvelines OFS, allows buyers to acquire a new home from an **entry price of 169,000 euros** on selected programs, by separating the building from the land via a 99-year lease. In Voisins-le-Bretonneux, L'Envol combines 50 % open-market and 50 % BRS units, a rare formula in the department. The programs meet RE 2020 standards, are eligible for the Zero-Rate Loan and come with off-plan sale guarantees (10-year structural, 2-year minor, completion guarantee).

### A differentiating commitment: art, quality of life and parity

The "1 building, 1 artwork" charter integrates a contemporary artwork in every delivered program, a signature unique to the Île-de-France developer landscape. The group is also a signatory of the gender parity charter in real estate. An online showroom lets buyers customise finishes before delivery. Interconstruction's operational capacity is also demonstrated by its co-development strategy on large-scale projects, already proven in the Hauts-de-Seine and progressively deployed in the 78.

## Les Nouveaux Constructeurs

**Les Nouveaux Constructeurs** has the densest portfolio in Yvelines with 9 programs in commercialisation for 2026. Established in 1969 and the author of more than 60,000 homes delivered in France, the group is positioned on the family residential and first-time buyer segments. The programs cover a wide range of towns: Le Clos de la Chênaie in Le Chesnay-Rocquencourt (from 275,400 euros), Les Jardins des Vergers in Poissy (213,900 euros), Les Jardins Foch in Orgeval (248,600 euros), Les Allées de la Chesnaie in Achères (257,900 euros), Domaine des Lys in Carrières-sous-Poissy (248,100 euros), Villas Péri in Saint-Cyr-l'École (211,000 euros), Les Allées du Roi in Versailles (635,000 euros), Carré des Arts in Sartrouville (169,000 euros) and Les Jardins d'Apolline in Plaisir (238,700 euros). The architecture favours contemporary volumes and human-scale residences, with deliveries scheduled between 2026 and 2028.

## Emerige

Founded in 2003, **Emerige** has historically focused on Paris and the inner suburbs. The developer intervenes occasionally in Yvelines on high-value architectural operations, notably in Saint-Germain-en-Laye and Le Vésinet. The positioning targets a discerning clientele on architectural signature (regular collaborations with renowned architects) and environmental quality of outdoor spaces (urban micro-forests, planted basins). The number of active programs in the 78 remains more modest than that of Les Nouveaux Constructeurs or Bouygues Immobilier, but the standing of each operation compensates for the rarity. ## Icade

A subsidiary of the Caisse des Dépôts et Consignations, **Icade** celebrated its 70th anniversary in 2024 and counts more than 100,000 homes delivered in France. In Yvelines, the developer operates mainly in Versailles, Saint-Cyr-l'École, Trappes and Mantes-la-Jolie, with a public-private partnership logic on urban renewal operations. Each program integrates a home automation solution and offers extensive finish customisation. Backing by a public institutional player is a strong argument for buyers seeking financial stability, particularly within a long-term investment logic. ## Bouygues Immobilier

**Bouygues Immobilier**, a subsidiary of the Bouygues group founded in 1956, has **8 programs in commercialisation in Yvelines** in 2026, with a strong concentration in Sartrouville where the developer runs more than 8 residences (VOLTIGE in BRS from 158,000 euros, Echo from 159,129 to 489,000 euros, IMPULSION at 279,575 euros, ESQUISSE, EMERGENCE, Aurore, SIENA ROCCA, EQUILIA). Saint-Germain-en-Laye hosts Le Jardin des Carmes (209,000 to 1,129,000 euros). Other towns complete the offering: Andrésy (Beloria), Les Clayes-sous-Bois (FAVORI), Hardricourt (Mira), Verneuil-sur-Seine, Poissy and Crespières. The price range spans from 158,000 to 1,129,000 euros, covering all segments. The financial solidity of the Bouygues group and the vertical integration with the group's construction subsidiaries guarantee the full coverage of constructor warranties (10-year structural, 2-year minor, completion guarantee).

## How to choose your real estate developer in Yvelines

### The 5 key criteria for comparing developers in the 78

Five criteria structure a rigorous comparison:

- **Geographic specialisation**: an exclusively Île-de-France player better masters Yvelines specifics (zoning, heritage constraints around Versailles and Saint-Germain-en-Laye, priority zones).
- **Town coverage**: number of towns covered in the 78, especially those concentrating demand (Versailles, Saint-Germain-en-Laye, Sartrouville).
- **Price positioning and subsidised schemes**: presence of BRS, 5.5 % VAT, PSLA, LLI programs, eligibility for the Zero-Rate Loan and the Jeanbrun scheme.
- **Program connectivity**: proximity to the RER A, Transilien L and N, future RER E (Eole) and Line 18 of the Grand Paris Express.
- **Architectural and environmental quality**: RE 2020 and NF Habitat HQE labels, quality of outdoor spaces and level of finish customisation.

### Which buyer profile suits each developer

Each developer targets a different buyer profile:

- First-time buyer or Île-de-France resident looking for a regional specialist with strong access to subsidised home ownership (BRS, 5.5 % VAT): Interconstruction.
- Buyer prioritising volume of available offer and diversity of towns: Les Nouveaux Constructeurs.
- Buyer demanding on architecture and standing, particularly in Saint-Germain-en-Laye and Le Vésinet: Emerige.
- Investor seeking institutional security and integrated home automation: Icade.
- Buyer targeting Sartrouville or Saint-Germain-en-Laye and attached to the solidity of a major construction group: Bouygues Immobilier.

### Mistakes to avoid

1. Failing to verify Pinel zoning and Zero-Rate Loan eligibility before reservation, which can cost several thousand euros in lost tax advantage.
2. Underestimating the real distance to the RER or Transilien (10 minutes on foot makes a genuine difference to valuation over 10 years).
3. Signing without comparing the offered notary fees, commercial discounts and bank application fee coverage conditions.
