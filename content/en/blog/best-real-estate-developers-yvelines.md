## Why buy a new-build home in Yvelines in 2026

### A department 32 % more expensive than the national average, sustained by transport

Yvelines shows a price per m2 **32 % above the French national average** (3,041 euros per m2 according to February 2025 data), with an average of 4,586 euros for apartments and 3,770 euros for houses. This valuation is driven by the quality of the network: RER A direct to Saint-Germain-en-Laye, Chatou and Le Vésinet, Transilien L and N, the Eole extension of the RER E to Mantes-la-Jolie scheduled for late 2026, and the future Line 18 of the Grand Paris Express connecting Versailles to Orly via the Saclay plateau. This density of infrastructure sustainably supports the valuation of new programs located less than 800 metres from a station.

### A family-oriented market: 77 % houses and 76.77 % owner-occupiers

The department counts **77.06 % houses and 76.77 % owner-occupiers**, making it the most family-oriented market in Île-de-France after the outer suburbs. However, new-build demand is still driven 90.5 % by apartment buildings, a sign that new households are looking for more affordable products than the standalone family home. For a broader trade-off on lifestyle, the guide [country or city: which to choose](/en/blog/country-or-city-choose/) details the decision criteria.

### Versailles, Saint-Germain-en-Laye and Sartrouville: three structuring hubs

According to trouver-un-logement-neuf.com, for the period from 22 May 2025 to 22 May 2026, Versailles concentrates 24.8 % of new-build buying intentions, followed by Saint-Germain-en-Laye (23.6 %) and Sartrouville (14.9 %). Together, these three towns represent 63.3 % of departmental demand. Versailles benefits from UNESCO heritage status and a dense tertiary employment base. Saint-Germain-en-Laye has direct RER A access. Sartrouville, more affordable in price, benefits from the Transilien L line to Paris Saint-Lazare and an active urban regeneration program.

## Reviews of real estate developers in Yvelines: what to look at in 2026

### Three criteria to interpret customer reviews

Reviews published on Google, Trustpilot or Avis Vérifiés vary significantly by program and sales agency. Three criteria should guide the reading:

- **On-time delivery**: a program delivered on time according to the reservation contract is rare in the sector (national average of 4 to 6 months of delay according to the Federation of Real Estate Developers). Developers with an average score above 4/5 on this criterion in the 78 include Interconstruction and Emerige.
- **Home conformity at delivery**: rate of reserves lifted within 30 days after handover. A reliable developer shows a rate above 85 %. Customer feedback on Hauts-de-Seine and the 78 places Interconstruction and Icade above average on this criterion.
- **After-sales service quality**: responsiveness on completion (1 year), minor (2 years) and 10-year structural warranty claims. Bouygues Immobilier benefits from the depth of the construction group for technical interventions.

### The 5 checks to perform before signing

Before signing a reservation contract with a developer in Yvelines, perform five checks:

1. Consult the company registration certificate and financial health of the developer on infogreffe.fr.
2. Read Google and Trustpilot reviews program by program and sales agency by sales agency (not just at group level).
3. Request the list of the developer's 5 most recent deliveries in the 78 and their rate of reserves lifted.
4. Verify membership in the Federation of Real Estate Developers (FPI) and a valid construction damage insurance subscription.
5. Request detailed off-plan sale guarantees (10-year structural, 2-year minor, completion guarantee) with insurer contact details and copies of certificates.

## How to choose the best real estate developer in Yvelines

### The 6 decision criteria in 2026

Six criteria structure a rigorous comparison:

- **Geographic specialisation**: an exclusively Île-de-France player better masters Yvelines specifics (zoning, heritage constraints, priority zones).
- **Town coverage**: number of towns covered in the department, especially those concentrating demand (Versailles, Saint-Germain-en-Laye, Sartrouville).
- **Price positioning**: presence of BRS, 5.5 % VAT, PSLA, LLI programs, eligibility for the Zero-Rate Loan and the Jeanbrun scheme.
- **Program connectivity**: proximity to the RER A, Transilien L and N, future RER E (Eole) and Line 18 of the Grand Paris Express.
- **Architectural and environmental quality**: RE 2020 and NF Habitat HQE labels, quality of outdoor spaces.
- **Customer reviews and delivery history**: average score per program, rate of reserves lifted, after-sales service responsiveness.

### The best developer based on your buyer profile

Each developer targets a different buyer profile:

- First-time buyer looking for subsidised home ownership (BRS, 5.5 % VAT) with a regional specialist: **Interconstruction**.
- Buyer prioritising volume of available offer and diversity of towns: **Les Nouveaux Constructeurs**.
- Buyer targeting Sartrouville or Saint-Germain-en-Laye and attached to a major construction group: **Bouygues Immobilier**.
- Buyer demanding on architecture and standing: **Emerige**.
- Investor seeking institutional security and integrated home automation: **Icade**.
