## Optimizing kitchen storage: basic principles

A well-organized kitchen is a working kitchen. **Optimizing kitchen storage** starts with an audit: what do I keep, what do I really use? A study shows that 30 percent of utensils in a French kitchen are never used.

### The work triangle rule

The triangle sink, stove, refrigerator must remain clear and short. Ideal distances are between 1.20 m and 2.70 m between each pole.

## Game-changing solutions

Deep drawers are the biggest progress of the last 20 years in kitchen storage. They allow access to the full depth of the unit without bending.

### Drawers vs cabinets comparison

| Criterion | Drawers | Cabinets |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| Ergonomics | Excellent | Average |
| Usable capacity | 80 to 90 percent | 60 to 70 percent |
| Additional cost | +15 to 25 percent | Reference |

## Exploiting every corner

Corner units are often poorly used. Layouts like turntables or magic corners allow reaching the back of the unit without contortion.

## Smart accessories

- Magnetic knife holder mounted on the wall
- Utensil bars above the countertop
- Shelves under high cabinets for spices
- Modular drawer dividers
- Built-in sorting baskets under the sink

### Organization method

1. Empty the kitchen entirely
2. Sort into 3 categories (keep, give, throw)
3. Group by usage zones
4. Assign a permanent location to each item

## Frequently asked questions

<details>
<summary>How to optimize storage in a small kitchen?</summary>

To optimize storage in a small kitchen, exploit ceiling height with high columns, install deep drawers (more practical than traditional cabinets) and use cabinet sides to mount utensil bars. You can gain up to 30 percent of storage space.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Drawers or cabinets: which to choose?</summary>

Drawers are more ergonomic: you can see and reach everything without bending. They are recommended for pots, plates and utensils. Cabinets remain useful for lighter items used less frequently.

</details>

<details>
<summary>How much does a well-designed kitchen cost?</summary>

A well-designed fitted kitchen costs between 3500 and 15000 euros depending on the range and surface. The average price of a custom kitchen is around 8000 euros for 10 sqm.

</details>
