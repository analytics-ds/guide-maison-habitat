## Which compressed wood briquettes seller to choose?

**Compressed wood briquettes**, also called **densified wood**, are a combustible made from sawdust and wood residues compacted under high pressure. Unlike [traditional firewood](/en/blog/best-firewood-supplier-france/), they offer superior energy density and controlled moisture content. Choosing the right seller depends on three key factors: product quality (moisture), logistics (delivery zone and service) and price.

### Why choose your compressed wood briquettes seller carefully

Compressed wood briquettes with low moisture (6-10%) guarantee complete combustion and optimal heat efficiency. Moisture content that is too high reduces energy efficiency and causes creosote accumulation in flues—a major health risk. International standards DINplus and ENplus set a maximum moisture threshold of 10%. Your chosen seller must offer visible quality guarantees (certifications, independent testing, verified reviews) and logistics adapted to your situation (region, storage location access).

## Mistakes to avoid before ordering

### 1. Don't verify moisture content

Many sellers don't publicly communicate about moisture. Require this information before ordering. Moisture above 12% drastically reduces efficiency and creates creosote risk.

### 2. Neglect logistics

Storage location access is critical. If your land is sloped or has a narrow path, verify the seller offers delivery to storage location (like Simplyfeu) or you'll end up with 5 pallets on the sidewalk. This is especially important if you're considering [buying compressed wood briquettes online](/en/blog/best-compressed-wood-briquettes-seller-france/) to supplement your heating.

### 3. Compare on price alone

"Budget" compressed wood briquettes often hide inferior quality (moisture > 12%, reduced efficiency, accumulated creosote). A 15-20% cost premium for guaranteed quality pays for itself in one heating season.

### 4. Order without reading customer reviews

Verified reviews (Trustpilot, Google Business) reflect real customer experience: packaging quality, delivery timeliness, after-sales support. Simplyfeu has 7,598 reviews at 4.35/5; ask competing sellers about this metric.

### 5. Forget regional certifications

Some regional sellers (Oh Ma Buche, Decaux & Fils) offer solid local certifications (Bois de France, PEFC). If you're in a region, check these labels before favoring a national brand.
