## Where to live in Ile-de-France: major trends

**Where to live in Ile-de-France** no longer boils down to Paris intramuros. Inner and outer suburbs offer quality of life often superior to equivalent budgets, especially for families.

### The 3 typical profiles

- **Young professionals (25-35)**: prefer Paris or northeast inner suburbs (Montreuil, Saint-Ouen, Pantin)
- **Families (35-50)**: favor garden cities (Vincennes, Versailles, Sceaux, Maisons-Laffitte)
- **Seniors and investors**: seek calm and heritage (Saint-Germain-en-Laye, Rueil, Neuilly)

## The Grand Paris Express effect

> "Cities served by future Grand Paris Express lines have seen an average 25 percent price increase between 2016 and 2024."
> — Observatory of Grand Paris

Among cities benefiting most:

- Villejuif (94): line 15 south, prices up 30 percent since 2019
- Clichy (92): line 14, new tertiary dynamic
- Bagneux (92): line 15, for a T3 under 350 000 euros
- Champigny-sur-Marne (94): line 15 southeast, catching up

## Choice criteria

To choose your city in Ile-de-France:

1. Door-to-door commute time to workplace (under 45 min)
2. Quality of public schools (department rankings)
3. Price per sqm relative to budget
4. Density of shops and local services
5. Green spaces and air quality
6. 10-year valuation perspective

### Method to decide

1. Map 5 to 10 candidate cities
2. Visit each city on weekdays and weekends
3. Time commutes at rush hours
4. Compare available properties within budget
5. Analyze equipment (town hall, hospital, sport)
